Virtual Museum

Women Speaks

"Women Speak" is a virtual museum that documents the evolution of women's rights and the feminist movement from ancient times to the present day. The installation allows visitors to navigate in a virtual 3D environment. Set against a backdrop of the starry sky, with a model of Earth at its center to symbolize the global sisterhood of women, players can navigate around the Earth using their mouse. Milestones in contrasting black and white record events where women's rights movements were advanced or prohibited within the same time frame. White walls display celebrated and encouraged events of the feminist movement, while black walls document instances where women's rights were exploited or prohibited.

A Woman Speaks

Audre Lorde

Moon marked and touched by sun

my magic is unwritten

but when the sea turns back

it will leave my shape behind.

I seek no favor

untouched by blood

unrelenting as the curse of love

permanent as my errors or my pride

I do not mix love with pity nor hate with scorn

and if you would know me

look into the entrails of Uranus

where the restless oceans pound.

I do not dwell

within my birth nor my divinities

who am ageless and half-grown

and still seeking

my sisters witches in Dahomey

wear me inside their coiled cloths

as our mother did

mourning.

I have been woman

for a long time

beware my smile

I am treacherous with old magic

and the noon's new fury

with all your wide futures

promised

I am woman

and not white.

Sources Collections

Timeline

Medieval
Use of Chastity Belts

Although historians debate whether chastity belts were widely used, they symbolize the control over female sexuality and bodies. Chastity belts were purportedly used to ensure a woman's fidelity, especially when their husbands were away.

11th to 20th Century
Foot Binding

Foot binding was a custom prevalent for nearly a thousand years in Chinese history, mainly among the Han Chinese society. It was seen as a symbol of beauty, delicacy, and nobility, and was a social standard for marriageable women. However, this practice caused tremendous harm to women's physical and mental health.

From the 15th to the 18th century
Witch Hunts

In Europe and North America, thousands of women were accused of being witches and suffered persecution, torture, and even execution. These so-called "witches" were often women who did not conform to traditional gender roles or were viewed as social outcasts.
